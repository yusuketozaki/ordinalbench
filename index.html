Abstract

Recently, Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have made remarkable progress, but challenges still remain in their ability to understand ordinal numbersâ€”a relatively easy task for humansâ€”especially concerning generalization to large-scale numbers and complex visual arrangements rarely seen in training data. Existing benchmarks have found it difficult to systematically evaluate the limits of VLM ordinal number understanding and generalization under these specific conditions. To address this gap, this research proposes OrdinalBench, a new benchmark dataset aimed at diagnostically evaluating the generalization limits of VLMs in ordinal number understanding. OrdinalBench primarily consists of an ``$N$th object identification task'' and includes programmatically and systematically generated 2D synthetic images (targeting "A00-Z99" string identifiers) and 3D synthetic images. This dataset systematically controls three main difficulty axes: (1) ``ordinal numbers,'' including those exceeding the total number of objects or extremely large numbers of 100 or more; (2) ``arrangement complexity,'' featuring ``single circular loops'' and ``maze-like loops''; and (3) ``the total number of objects.'' Through zero-shot evaluation of existing VLMs, OrdinalBench provides a diagnostic tool to quantitatively reveal their performance limits under these challenging conditions and identify model weaknesses. Furthermore, it attempts to verify structured intermediate step outputs to improve the interpretability of reasoning processes. This dataset, consisting of test data only, is planned for public release, and aims to contribute to the development of more robust and generalizable VLMs.
